# OpsFlow Ubiquitous Language & Domain Dictionary

As noted in Domain-Driven Design, bridging the language gap between the domain expert and the AI is critical. Providing this shared dictionary ensures the AI uses the exact same terminology when writing variables, component names, and data models.

## Task Management & App Architecture (Zenput Inspired)

*   **Open Task**: A checklist item or task that is scheduled without a specific employee assigned to it; any available store-level employee can "grab" it by entering their name and completing it.
*   **Corrective Action**: An automated follow-up task that triggers immediately when a primary task is missed, done incorrectly, or flags an issue (e.g., a customer injury report), notifying the manager automatically.
*   **Manager Dashboard**: The remote oversight interface where managers can view real-time execution data, track overall store performance, and identify top issues for coaching.
*   **Proof of Work**: Photos or form inputs attached to a task by an employee to verify a checklist item was completed correctly.

## Restaurant Operations Context (from Standard Procedures)

*   **MDOG**: The Make Line Dough Optimization Guide/Prep Sheet used for determining hourly dough pulls and daily prep needs.
*   **Make Line**: The primary food assembly area that must be fully stocked, equipped with portion cups, and date-labeled according to the MDOG.
*   **Lexans**: The food storage containers located in the top and bottom wells of the make line that must be cleaned and rotated.
*   **Walk-in**: The main refrigerated storage area where inventory is managed and dough temperature (e.g., bringing it back in when it reaches 56 degrees) is tightly controlled.
*   **Slap Station**: The dough stretching and preparation counter.
*   **Dustinator**: The flour or dusting mixture used at the slap station, which frequently needs to be swept and wiped down during closing.
*   **Till A / Till B**: The specific cash drawers managed during opening, shift changes, and closing cash audits.
